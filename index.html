Overview

3D perception of deformable objects is critical for industrial, household, and healthcare applications, yet progress has been hindered by the lack of suitable datasets. To address this, we introduce FabricDeform3D (FD3D), a dataset of 37,500 annotated point clouds and corresponding meshes across 15 classes of highly deformable objects, such as clothing and bags, with 65 semantic part labels. While geometric deep learning methods have excelled on rigid point clouds by exploiting local geometric structure, they struggle with deformable objects undergoing continuous, non-rigid transformations. Therefore, we also introduce TopoMetric Graph Neural Network (TOM GNN), a novel approach inspired by topological data analysis. TOM GNN computes geometric features from topological structures, enabling it to capture global connectivity patterns that remain invariant to deformations. This work focuses on semantic part segmentation because of its importance in tasks like robotic laundry folding and automated grocery bagging, and its well-established foundation in rigid object research that enables robust benchmarking. On FD3D, TOM GNN outperforms the strongest baseline by 2.1% mean IoU, establishing a new state-of-the-art.
